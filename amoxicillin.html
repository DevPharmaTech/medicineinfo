💊 Medicine Information: Amoxicillin

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Amoxicillin

💊 Dosage Form: Capsule / Tablet / Syrup

⚖️ Strength: 250 mg, 500 mg, 125 mg/5ml (syrup)

🏭 Manufacturer: GSK, Cipla, Abbott, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Treats bacterial infections

  • Respiratory tract infections
  • Ear infections (otitis media)
  • Throat infections (tonsillitis, pharyngitis)
  • Urinary tract infections (UTIs)
  • Skin infections
  • Dental abscess

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Amoxicillin is a **penicillin-type antibiotic**.

🔧 It works by **inhibiting bacterial cell wall synthesis**, causing the bacteria to rupture and die.

📏 Dosage

👨‍🦱 Adults:
• 250–500 mg every 8 hours (based on infection type and severity)

🧒 Children:
• Dose based on weight (typically 20–40 mg/kg/day in divided doses)

💧 Syrup form is used for children or those who can’t swallow tablets.

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 🤢 Nausea, vomiting
  • 💨 Diarrhea
  • ❗ Skin rash
  • 🚨 Rare: Allergic reaction (anaphylaxis)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ⚠️ Check for **penicillin allergy** before use
  • ❗ May cause diarrhea — stay hydrated
  • 📞 Call doctor if rash, breathing difficulty, or severe reaction occurs

❄️ Storage

📦 Store tablets/capsules at room temperature

🧴 Refrigerate syrup (if instructed)

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Proven effective for a wide range of bacterial infections

🔍 Studied for use in ENT, dental, and urinary infections

🌍 Included in WHO Essential Medicines List

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ May reduce effectiveness of oral contraceptives
  • ❗ Increased rash risk when taken with allopurinol
  • ⚠️ Use with caution with methotrexate
  • 🍺 Alcohol is generally safe, but avoid excess during infection
