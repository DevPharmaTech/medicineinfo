💊 Medicine Information: Atorvastatin

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Atorvastatin

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet

⚖️ Strength: 10 mg, 20 mg, 40 mg, 80 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: Pfizer, Sun Pharma, Cipla, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used to manage:

  • High cholesterol (hyperlipidemia)
  • Prevention of cardiovascular events
  • Familial hypercholesterolemia
  • Prevention of stroke and heart attack

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Atorvastatin is a **statin** (HMG-CoA reductase inhibitor).

🛑 It blocks an enzyme involved in cholesterol synthesis in the liver, thereby **reducing LDL (bad cholesterol)** and **increasing HDL (good cholesterol)**.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults:
• Start with 10–20 mg once daily
• Can be increased to 80 mg based on response and lipid levels

📅 Taken once daily, with or without food

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 💢 Muscle pain or weakness (myopathy)
  • 🧠 Headache
  • 🍃 Digestive issues (nausea, constipation)
  • ⚠️ Rare: Liver enzyme elevation, rhabdomyolysis

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • 🧪 Monitor liver function tests
  • ❌ Avoid in active liver disease
  • ⚠️ Caution in patients with renal impairment
  • 🥤 Avoid excessive alcohol

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (15°C – 30°C)

🌥️ Protect from light and moisture

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

📊 Proven in major studies (e.g., TNT, ASCOT) to significantly reduce cardiovascular risk.

🧪 Long-term use shows effective control of lipid levels and prevention of heart disease.

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ May interact with CYP3A4 inhibitors (e.g., erythromycin, ketoconazole)
  • 🚫 Grapefruit juice increases blood levels and side effects
  • 💊 Use caution with other lipid-lowering agents like fibrates
  • 🩺 Possible interaction with warfarin (increased bleeding risk)
