💊 Medicine Information: Domperidone

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Domperidone

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet / Suspension

⚖️ Strength: 10 mg (tablet), 1 mg/ml (syrup)

🏭 Manufacturer: Torrent, Cipla, Sun Pharma, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

  • 🤢 Nausea and vomiting
  • 🧬 Gastroparesis (delayed stomach emptying)
  • ⚠️ Acid reflux and indigestion (as adjuvant)
  • 🍼 Also used to promote lactation (under medical advice)

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

⚡ Blocks **dopamine D2 receptors** in the brain and gut

🔄 Increases **gastric motility** and speeds up stomach emptying

⛔ Prevents nausea signals from reaching vomiting center

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults: 10 mg 3 times a day, 15–30 minutes before meals

🧒 Children: Dose adjusted by weight (doctor's supervision)

⏱️ Short-term use (usually not more than 7–14 days)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 🌀 Dizziness
  • 🍽️ Dry mouth
  • 🔄 Abdominal cramps
  • ❤️ Rare: Irregular heartbeat or QT prolongation
  • 🧬 Hormonal changes (in long-term/high dose)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❌ Not recommended for patients with heart rhythm problems
  • 💓 Use caution in elderly or those with liver issues
  • ⚠️ Should not be combined with certain antibiotics (e.g., erythromycin)
  • 📋 Always use for shortest duration possible

❄️ Storage

🌡️ Store below 25°C in a cool, dry place

🚫 Protect from direct sunlight

🧴 Keep syrup tightly closed

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Proven to reduce symptoms of nausea and gastric discomfort

📈 Effective in improving gut motility in diabetic gastroparesis

⚠️ Long-term safety concerns have limited use duration

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • 💊 Avoid with **ketoconazole**, **erythromycin** (risk of arrhythmia)
  • ⚠️ Interacts with **antacids** and **proton pump inhibitors**
  • 🧪 Use caution with liver enzyme inhibitors
