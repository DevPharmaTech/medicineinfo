💊 Medicine Information: Pantoprazole

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Pantoprazole

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet (delayed release), Injection

⚖️ Strength: 20 mg, 40 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: Cipla, Sun Pharma, Dr. Reddy’s, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used to treat:

  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Peptic ulcers
  • Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
  • Stomach ulcers caused by NSAIDs

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🧬 Pantoprazole is a **Proton Pump Inhibitor (PPI)**

🔬 It blocks the **H+/K+ ATPase enzyme** in the stomach lining, reducing gastric acid secretion.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults: 20–40 mg once daily, before meals

🧒 Children: Based on age/weight (under medical supervision)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😐 Headache
  • 🤢 Nausea, abdominal pain
  • 💨 Bloating, gas, diarrhea
  • ⚠️ Rare: Vitamin B12 deficiency (long-term), low magnesium

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ⏳ Long-term use may lead to nutrient deficiencies
  • 🦴 May increase fracture risk in elderly (with long-term use)
  • ❗ Should be taken **before meals**
  • 🔄 Monitor for rebound acid production after stopping

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (15–30°C)

🚫 Protect from light and moisture

🔬 Clinical Trials

📊 Proven effective in reducing acid reflux symptoms and healing ulcers

🔎 Clinical trials support safety profile for both short-term and long-term use (with monitoring)

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ Reduces absorption of drugs like ketoconazole, atazanavir
  • 💊 May interact with warfarin (monitor INR)
  • 🩺 Can reduce effectiveness of clopidogrel (caution advised)
