💊 Medicine Information: Metformin

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Metformin Hydrochloride

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet (Immediate/Extended Release)

⚖️ Strength: 500 mg, 850 mg, 1000 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: USV, Sun Pharma, Cipla, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used to control blood sugar levels in:

  • Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus
  • Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS)
  • Insulin resistance-related disorders

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Metformin reduces **hepatic glucose production**.

🚪 Increases **insulin sensitivity** and glucose uptake in muscles.

💉 Does not stimulate insulin release — thus low risk of hypoglycemia.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults:
• Initial: 500–850 mg once/twice daily with meals
• Max: 2000–2500 mg/day (in divided doses)

🕒 Extended Release: Once daily with evening meal

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😖 Nausea, vomiting
  • 🌪️ Diarrhea
  • 🤢 Metallic taste
  • ⚠️ Rare: Lactic acidosis (serious and life-threatening)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❌ Avoid in kidney/liver impairment
  • 🚫 Stop before contrast imaging (iodine dye)
  • 📞 Monitor Vitamin B12 during long-term use

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature below 25°C

🌥️ Protect from moisture and sunlight

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Widely studied for diabetes and metabolic syndrome

📈 Reduces HbA1c levels effectively

💡 Shown to have cardiovascular protective benefits

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ Increased risk of lactic acidosis with alcohol
  • ⚠️ May interact with cimetidine, diuretics
  • 🧪 May reduce absorption of Vitamin B12
