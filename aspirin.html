💊 Medicine Information: Aspirin

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Aspirin (Acetylsalicylic Acid)

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet / Chewable / Effervescent

⚖️ Strength: 75 mg, 325 mg, 500 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: Bayer, Cipla, Sun Pharma, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes (for high doses or long-term use)

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Pain relief and anti-inflammatory
✅ Reduces risk of heart attack and stroke
✅ Used for:

  • Fever
  • Muscle aches
  • Joint pain (arthritis)
  • Heart disease prevention (low dose)
  • Blood thinning after heart attack

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔧 Inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, which reduces production of prostaglandins (chemicals responsible for pain, fever, and inflammation).

❤️ At low doses, it prevents blood clot formation by inhibiting platelet aggregation.

📏 Dosage

👨‍🦱 Adults:
• Pain relief: 325–650 mg every 4–6 hours
• Heart protection: 75–100 mg daily

❌ Not recommended for children (due to Reye’s Syndrome risk)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 🤢 Nausea, stomach pain, heartburn
  • 🩸 Increased risk of bleeding
  • ❗ Allergic reactions (rash, asthma, swelling)
  • 🚨 Reye’s syndrome in children (rare but serious)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❌ Avoid in children under 16 with viral illness
  • ⚠️ Use caution in ulcers or GI bleeding history
  • ❗ Avoid combining with other blood thinners
  • 🚫 Not for use before surgery unless advised

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (below 25°C)
🌥️ Protect from heat and moisture
🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

🩺 Widely studied for cardiovascular benefits and anti-inflammatory properties.

✅ Proven to reduce heart attacks and strokes in high-risk patients.

🌍 Included in the WHO Essential Medicines List for heart protection.

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ Increases risk of bleeding with Warfarin, Heparin
  • ⚠️ Interaction with NSAIDs (e.g. Ibuprofen)
  • ⚠️ May reduce effectiveness of some blood pressure drugs
  • ❗ Avoid alcohol (increased stomach bleeding risk)
