💊 Medicine Information: Ibuprofen

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Ibuprofen

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet / Suspension / Gel

⚖️ Strength: 200 mg, 400 mg, 600 mg, 800 mg (tablet)

🏭 Manufacturer: Abbott, Cipla, GSK, and more

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes (for higher strengths)

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Pain relief
✅ Reduces inflammation
✅ Lowers fever

  • Headache
  • Back pain
  • Arthritis
  • Toothache
  • Menstrual cramps
  • Muscle pain

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🧠 Inhibits COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes, reducing prostaglandin production
🔧 Result: Less inflammation, pain, and fever

📏 Dosage

👨‍🦱 Adults:
• 200–400 mg every 4–6 hours
• Max: 1200 mg/day (OTC) or 3200 mg/day (under medical supervision)

🧒 Children:
• 5–10 mg/kg every 6–8 hours
• Max 4 doses per day (doctor guidance required)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😐 Common: Stomach upset, nausea, dizziness
  • 🚨 Serious (rare): Stomach ulcers, bleeding, kidney damage

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❌ Avoid in patients with peptic ulcers or GI bleeding
  • ❗ Caution in kidney or heart conditions
  • ⚠️ Do not combine with alcohol or other NSAIDs
  • 🔒 Take with food or milk to reduce stomach upset

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (15–30°C)
🌥️ Keep in a dry place
🚫 Keep away from children

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Proven effective in managing mild to moderate pain and inflammation
🧪 Widely studied for arthritis, injury pain, menstrual pain
🌍 Commonly included in global pain management protocols

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ Increased risk of bleeding with anticoagulants (e.g., warfarin)
  • ⚠️ May reduce effect of antihypertensives (e.g., ACE inhibitors)
  • ⚠️ Risk of kidney damage with diuretics or other NSAIDs
  • ⚠️ Avoid combining with aspirin unless advised
