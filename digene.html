💊 Medicine Information: Digene / Antacids

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Aluminum Hydroxide + Magnesium Hydroxide + Simethicone

💊 Dosage Form: Chewable Tablet / Oral Suspension

⚖️ Strength: Varies by formulation

🏭 Manufacturer: Abbott, Pfizer, others

📝 Prescription Required: ❌ No (Over-the-counter)

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Provides relief from:

  • Acidity
  • Heartburn
  • Indigestion
  • Gas and bloating
  • Acid reflux

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔹 Aluminum & Magnesium Hydroxide: Neutralize excess stomach acid

🔹 Simethicone: Breaks up gas bubbles to relieve bloating

📉 Together, they reduce acidity, relieve gas, and soothe the stomach lining.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults: 2 teaspoons (10 ml) or 1–2 tablets, after meals and at bedtime

🧒 Children: As directed by physician

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 💨 Bloating or belching
  • 😐 Constipation (from aluminum)
  • 💩 Diarrhea (from magnesium)
  • ⚠️ Prolonged use may affect calcium/magnesium levels

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❗ Use with caution in kidney patients
  • 🧂 May interfere with absorption of other medicines (take 1-2 hours apart)
  • 🚫 Not for long-term use without doctor advice
  • ⏱️ Avoid taking more than 8 tablets or 60ml per day unless prescribed

❄️ Storage

🌡️ Store at room temperature (15–30°C)

🚫 Keep away from children and moisture

🔬 Clinical Trials

📊 Proven to relieve heartburn and acid indigestion within 15–30 minutes

🔎 Well tolerated in short-term use for gastric relief

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ May reduce absorption of iron, tetracyclines, fluoroquinolones
  • 🕑 Take other meds at least 1–2 hours apart
  • ⚖️ Monitor magnesium/aluminum levels in long-term use
