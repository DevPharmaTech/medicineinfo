💊 Medicine Information: Omeprazole

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Omeprazole

💊 Dosage Form: Capsule / Tablet / Oral Suspension

⚖️ Strength: 10 mg, 20 mg, 40 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: Dr. Reddy’s, Sun Pharma, Cipla, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ❌ No (available OTC in many countries)

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used to treat conditions related to excess stomach acid:

  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Peptic ulcer disease
  • Heartburn
  • Helicobacter pylori infections (as part of combination therapy)
  • Zollinger–Ellison syndrome

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Omeprazole is a **proton pump inhibitor (PPI)**.

🚫 Blocks the **H⁺/K⁺-ATPase** enzyme in the stomach lining.

🛑 This reduces **gastric acid secretion** and relieves acid-related conditions.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults:
• GERD: 20 mg once daily for 4–8 weeks
• Ulcers: 20–40 mg once daily
• H. pylori: 20 mg twice daily (with antibiotics)

📅 Should be taken before meals, preferably in the morning.

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😐 Headache
  • 🌪️ Diarrhea or constipation
  • 😴 Fatigue
  • ⚠️ Long-term use: Risk of vitamin B12 deficiency, osteoporosis, and low magnesium

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❗ Use cautiously in long-term therapy
  • 🩸 May increase risk of **C. difficile infection**
  • ⚠️ Can mask symptoms of stomach cancer
  • 📉 Monitor in patients with liver issues

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature below 25°C

🌥️ Protect from moisture and light

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Proven effective in acid suppression therapy

🧪 Long-term studies show relief in GERD and ulcer prevention

📊 Widely used globally with good safety profile

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ May reduce absorption of drugs like ketoconazole, iron, vitamin B12
  • ⚠️ Affects levels of drugs metabolized by CYP2C19 (e.g. diazepam, clopidogrel)
  • 🩺 Can interact with warfarin, methotrexate, digoxin
