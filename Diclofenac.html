💊 Medicine Information: Diclofenac

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Diclofenac

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet, Gel, Injection

⚖️ Strength: 25 mg, 50 mg, 75 mg, 100 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: Multiple (e.g., Novartis, Cipla, Sun Pharma)

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used for:

  • Rheumatoid arthritis
  • Osteoarthritis
  • Muscle pain and back pain
  • Dysmenorrhea (painful periods)
  • Postoperative pain

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Diclofenac is a **non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drug (NSAID)**.

🧬 It works by **inhibiting COX-1 and COX-2 enzymes**, reducing prostaglandin synthesis, thereby lowering inflammation and pain.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults: 50 mg to 100 mg 2–3 times daily depending on severity

🧒 Pediatric: Based on weight and indication (consult physician)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 🤢 Nausea, vomiting
  • 😵 Dizziness, headache
  • 🧠 Drowsiness
  • 🔥 Heartburn, stomach upset
  • ⚠️ Serious (rare): GI bleeding, liver toxicity, kidney issues

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❗ Not for long-term use without supervision
  • ⚠️ Use with caution in ulcers, kidney or liver disease
  • ❤️ Increases risk of heart attack/stroke with prolonged use
  • 🤰 Avoid in third trimester of pregnancy

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at 15–30°C

🌥️ Protect from heat, light, and moisture

🔬 Clinical Trials

📊 Clinical studies confirm diclofenac's strong anti-inflammatory effects, comparable to ibuprofen and naproxen.

🧪 Proven effective in arthritis and musculoskeletal pain, but carries GI and cardiovascular risk.

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • 💊 Increased bleeding risk with warfarin, aspirin
  • 💥 May reduce effect of antihypertensives (e.g., ACE inhibitors)
  • ⚠️ Use with methotrexate or lithium can increase toxicity
  • 🍺 Avoid alcohol – increases risk of stomach bleeding
