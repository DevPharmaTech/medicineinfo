💨 Medicine Information: Salbutamol

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Salbutamol

💊 Dosage Form: Inhaler, Tablet, Syrup, Nebulizer Solution

⚖️ Strength: 100 mcg (inhaler), 2 mg & 4 mg (tablet), 2 mg/5ml (syrup)

🏭 Manufacturer: Cipla, GlaxoSmithKline (Ventolin), Lupin, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used to relieve:

  • Bronchial asthma
  • Chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD)
  • Acute bronchospasm
  • Exercise-induced asthma

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🧬 Salbutamol is a **short-acting beta-2 adrenergic agonist (SABA)**.

💨 It relaxes bronchial smooth muscle, leading to **bronchodilation** and improved airflow in the lungs.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Inhaler: 1–2 puffs every 4–6 hours as needed

🧒 Children (Syrup/Tablets): Dose based on weight and age (consult a physician)

💧 Nebulizer: 2.5–5 mg up to 4 times daily

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 🫀 Palpitations
  • 😰 Tremors or nervousness
  • 💥 Headache
  • 😴 Insomnia
  • 🫁 Throat irritation (inhaler)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ⚠️ Use with caution in patients with heart conditions
  • 💊 Do not exceed recommended dose – may cause serious cardiac effects
  • 🤰 Use in pregnancy only if clearly needed
  • 🫁 Rinse mouth after inhaler use to avoid irritation

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (15°C – 30°C)

🌥️ Keep away from moisture, heat, and direct sunlight

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

🧪 Salbutamol has been widely studied and is approved for rapid relief of bronchospasm.

📈 Demonstrated efficacy in improving pulmonary function in asthma and COPD patients.

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • 💔 Beta-blockers (e.g., propranolol) may reduce its effectiveness
  • 🫀 Use with other sympathomimetics may increase cardiovascular effects
  • 🧪 May interact with MAO inhibitors and tricyclic antidepressants
  • 🧬 Monitor potassium levels when used with diuretics
