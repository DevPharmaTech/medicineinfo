💊 Medicine Information: Levocetirizine

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Levocetirizine

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet / Syrup

⚖️ Strength: 5 mg (tablet), 2.5 mg/5ml (syrup)

🏭 Manufacturer: Cipla, Alkem, Sun Pharma, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

  • 🤧 Allergic rhinitis (runny nose, sneezing)
  • 🌸 Seasonal allergies (hay fever)
  • 🌿 Chronic urticaria (hives, skin allergies)
  • 😴 Night-time allergy symptoms

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🧬 Blocks **H1 histamine receptors**, preventing allergic reactions

🛡️ Reduces release of histamine, controlling allergy symptoms

🎯 Acts selectively with fewer drowsy effects than older antihistamines

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Adults & children over 12: 5 mg once daily (preferably at night)

🧒 Children 6–12 years: 2.5 mg once or twice daily (based on doctor's advice)

⚠️ Not recommended for children under 6 unless prescribed

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😴 Drowsiness (less than cetirizine)
  • 🌀 Dizziness
  • 😵 Dry mouth
  • 💤 Fatigue

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • 🚗 May cause drowsiness – avoid driving or operating machinery
  • ❗ Use with caution in kidney or liver disease
  • 🚫 Avoid alcohol consumption
  • 📋 Consult doctor if using other sedative medications

❄️ Storage

🌡️ Store at room temperature (below 30°C)

🧴 Protect from light and moisture

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

📊 Proven effective in reducing allergy symptoms like sneezing and itching

🧪 Shown to be safer with fewer sedative effects compared to older antihistamines

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • 💊 May enhance drowsiness with other CNS depressants
  • ⚠️ Interacts with alcohol, sedatives, and sleeping pills
  • 🔍 Always inform your doctor about other medications
