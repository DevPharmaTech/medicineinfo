💊 Medicine Information: Cetirizine

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Cetirizine Hydrochloride

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet / Syrup

⚖️ Strength: 10 mg (tablet), 5 mg/5ml (syrup)

🏭 Manufacturer: Dr. Reddy's, Cipla, Sun Pharma, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ❌ No (OTC in many countries)

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used to relieve allergy symptoms

  • Hay fever (allergic rhinitis)
  • Sneezing, runny or itchy nose
  • Watery or itchy eyes
  • Skin allergies like urticaria (hives)

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Cetirizine is a **second-generation antihistamine**.

🔧 It blocks **H1 histamine receptors**, preventing allergic symptoms like itching, swelling, and nasal congestion.

📏 Dosage

👨‍🦱 Adults & Children (≥6 yrs):
• 10 mg once daily

🧒 Children 2–5 years:
• 2.5–5 mg once daily (consult pediatrician)

🌙 Best taken at night if drowsiness occurs

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😴 Drowsiness
  • 💨 Dry mouth
  • 🤕 Headache
  • ❗ Fatigue or dizziness (rare)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ⚠️ May cause drowsiness — avoid driving or alcohol
  • ❗ Use cautiously in kidney or liver impairment
  • 📞 Inform doctor if taken regularly or during pregnancy

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (below 25°C)

🌥️ Keep away from moisture and sunlight

🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Proven effective in seasonal and chronic allergies

📊 Compared favorably to older antihistamines (less drowsy)

🔬 Used in allergy research and long-term safety studies

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ Increased drowsiness with alcohol or sedatives
  • ⚠️ May interact with other CNS depressants
  • ❗ Minimal drug interactions overall
