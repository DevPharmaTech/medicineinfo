💊 Medicine Information: Paracetamol

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Paracetamol

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet / Syrup / Injection

⚖️ Strength: 500 mg (tablet), 250 mg/5ml (syrup)

🏭 Manufacturer: GSK, Cipla, Sun Pharma, etc.

📝 Prescription Required: ❌ No (Over-the-counter)

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Reduces fever
✅ Relieves mild to moderate pain

  • Headache
  • Muscle pain
  • Toothache
  • Cold & flu symptoms
  • Menstrual cramps

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🧠 Acts in the central nervous system
🔧 Inhibits COX enzymes, lowering prostaglandins
🔥 Reduces pain and fever

📏 Dosage

👨‍🦱 Adults:
• 500–1000 mg every 4–6 hours
• Max: 4000 mg (4g) per day

🧒 Children:
• 10–15 mg/kg every 6 hours
• Based on weight (doctor consultation recommended)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 😐 Common: Nausea, stomach upset
  • 🚨 Serious (rare): Liver damage (especially with overdose or alcohol)

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ❌ Avoid alcohol
  • ❗ Not suitable for people with liver or kidney disease
  • 🔒 Do not exceed the recommended dose
  • 📞 Call doctor if pain/fever lasts more than 3 days

❄️ Storage

📦 Store below 25°C
🌥️ Keep in a cool, dry place
🚫 Keep out of reach of children

🔬 Clinical Trials

✅ Extensively tested for safety & effectiveness
🌍 Part of WHO Essential Medicines List
🩻 Works well for fever & pain
💡 Gentler on the stomach than NSAIDs

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • ⚠️ Can increase bleeding risk with Warfarin
  • ⚠️ Liver toxicity risk with alcohol
  • ⚠️ May interact with Phenytoin, Carbamazepine, and other liver-metabolized drugs
