💊 Medicine Information: Ranitidine

📌 Basic Information

🧪 Generic Name: Ranitidine

💊 Dosage Form: Tablet, Syrup, Injection

⚖️ Strength: 150 mg, 300 mg

🏭 Manufacturer: Various (Note: Withdrawn in many countries)

📝 Prescription Required: ✅ Yes

🩺 Uses / Indications

✅ Used for:

  • Peptic ulcer disease
  • Gastroesophageal reflux disease (GERD)
  • Zollinger-Ellison syndrome
  • Heartburn relief

⚙️ Mechanism of Action

🔬 Ranitidine is an **H2-receptor antagonist**.

🧬 It works by **blocking histamine H2 receptors** in stomach lining, reducing acid production.

📏 Dosage

👨‍⚕️ Typical: 150 mg twice daily or 300 mg at bedtime

🧒 Pediatric: Adjusted per weight and indication (consult doctor)

⚠️ Side Effects

  • 🤢 Nausea
  • 😵 Dizziness
  • 🧠 Headache
  • 💤 Fatigue
  • 🧬 Rare: Blood count changes, liver enzyme abnormalities

🚫 Warnings / Precautions

  • ⚠️ Use cautiously in kidney/liver impairment
  • 📉 May mask symptoms of stomach cancer
  • ⛔ Withdrawn in many countries due to contamination risk (NDMA)
  • 🤰 Pregnancy category B – use only if clearly needed

❄️ Storage

📦 Store at room temperature (15°C – 30°C)

🌥️ Keep in a dry, cool place away from sunlight

🔬 Clinical Trials

📊 Ranitidine was proven effective in acid-related disorders, but later trials raised concerns due to NDMA impurity formation under storage.

🧪 Resulted in global recalls and discontinuation in many countries (like USA & India).

🔄 Drug Interactions

  • 💊 May reduce absorption of drugs requiring acidic pH (e.g., ketoconazole, atazanavir)
  • 💥 May interact with warfarin (monitor INR)
  • ⚠️ Avoid use with alcohol – may worsen side effects
  • 🧬 Minimal CYP450 interaction
